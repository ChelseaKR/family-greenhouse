# Repotting houseplants

Repotting is less frequent than people assume. Most houseplants need it every 1-3 years, not every season. And bigger isn't always better — going up too many sizes is a fast way to cause root rot.

## When to repot

Repot when:

- Roots are growing out the drainage holes
- Water runs straight through the pot and out the bottom (the root ball has displaced the soil)
- The plant has stopped growing despite good light + water
- You see roots circling around the inside of the pot when you slide it out
- The soil compacts down significantly between waterings (no aeration left)

DON'T repot when:

- The plant is flowering — wait until after
- The plant is stressed, sick, or recovering from anything
- It's mid-winter (most houseplants don't grow then)
- You just brought the plant home — wait 2-3 weeks for it to acclimate

Best season to repot is early spring, right before the growth spurt.

## How to size up

Go up by one pot size. A 4" plant goes into a 5" or 6" pot, not a 10". Excess soil holds excess water around roots that can't reach it; that water sits stagnant and breeds rot.

The new pot should be 1-2 inches wider in diameter than the old one. That's it.

## Step-by-step

1. **Water the plant a day before** repotting. A hydrated root ball comes out cleanly; a dry one shatters.
2. **Prepare the new pot**: drainage hole confirmed, a thin layer of fresh soil at the bottom.
3. **Slide the plant out** of the old pot by tilting it sideways and squeezing the sides. Don't yank.
4. **Loosen the root ball gently**. If roots are tightly wound, tease them apart with your fingers — it stimulates outward growth into the new soil. Trim any dead/brown roots.
5. **Set the plant in the new pot** at the same depth it was in the old one. Don't bury the crown.
6. **Fill in with fresh soil** around the sides. Press lightly — don't pack hard. You want soil contact, not compacted soil.
7. **Water thoroughly**, let it drain. Wait at least 4 weeks before fertilizing.

## Soil mix

Don't use generic "potting soil" straight from the bag for most houseplants — it's too dense. A good basic mix:

- 60% potting soil
- 30% perlite or pumice (for drainage + aeration)
- 10% orchid bark or coco coir (for chunkier structure)

Adjustments by plant type:

- **Succulents/cacti**: 40% potting soil + 50% perlite + 10% coarse sand. Drains in seconds.
- **Aroids (monsteras, philodendrons)**: add more orchid bark — they're epiphytes and like chunky, airy mix.
- **African violets**: pre-mixed African violet soil is fine; it's a fluffier blend than standard.
- **Orchids**: never use potting soil. Use bark-based orchid mix.

## After repotting

The plant may droop for a few days. That's normal — root disturbance temporarily reduces water uptake. Don't compensate with more water.

Hold off on fertilizer for 4-6 weeks. Fresh soil has enough nutrients to start. Hold off on direct sun for a week.

If a leaf or two yellows in the first week, that's also normal stress shedding. Let it run its course.
