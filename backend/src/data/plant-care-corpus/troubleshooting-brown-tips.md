# Brown leaf tips — what they actually mean

Brown leaf tips are the second-most-common houseplant complaint after yellow leaves. They're almost always one of three things, and they're almost never caused by the thing people guess first (overwatering).

## Cause 1: Low humidity (most common)

Plants from rainforest environments crisp at the tips when ambient humidity drops below ~40%. The tips dry out faster than the plant can rehydrate them.

Most likely culprits:

- Calatheas, marantas, prayer plants: even 50% humidity isn't enough; they want 60-70%
- Ferns: maidenhairs are notorious; Boston ferns less so but still demanding
- Dracaenas, especially Janet Craig and Lemon Lime
- Spider plants in winter
- Peace lilies in dry heating-season air

The tell: tips are crispy-dry like paper, not soft. The brown is hard, not mushy. The edges may also be crispy a bit further in.

Fix: humidifier. Mist trays and misting don't really work for this; the only reliable solution is to add water vapor to the air with a humidifier set to 50%+.

## Cause 2: Salt buildup in soil (water quality)

Tap water with high mineral content (chlorine, fluoride, dissolved solids) leaves residue in the soil. The roots can't filter all of it, so excess salts move up into the leaves and concentrate at the tips, burning them.

Most likely if:

- You water with cold tap water in a hard-water area
- You fertilize regularly without flushing the pot
- The pot has a white crust on the rim or soil surface
- Multiple plants are getting brown tips at once

Fix: flush the soil. Run water through the pot 3-4 times its volume to wash out accumulated salts. Switch to filtered water or rainwater. Skip 1-2 fertilizer cycles. Re-flush every 3-6 months.

Particularly sensitive plants: calatheas, dracaenas, spider plants, prayer plants. These want rainwater or filtered water, not tap.

## Cause 3: Inconsistent watering

Letting a plant go bone-dry between waterings, then drenching it, then going bone-dry again — the swings damage root tips, and the damage shows up as leaf-tip dieback.

The tell: tips are brown but the rest of the leaf looks healthy. The plant gets browning tips every few weeks despite no obvious cause.

Fix: be more consistent. Water before the plant goes completely dry. Use a moisture meter if you can't read the soil reliably. For sensitive plants, bottom watering is more consistent than top watering.

## Cause 4 (less common): Over-fertilizing

Fertilizer salts are the same problem as tap-water salts, just from a different source. The symptoms and fix are identical to cause 2.

If you fertilize at full strength weekly, that's the problem. Drop to half strength every 2-3 weeks and flush the pot.

## What to do with the brown tips

Once a tip is brown, it's not coming back — the leaf can't re-green dead tissue. You have two options:

1. **Leave them alone** if it's just a couple of mm. The brown is harmless, just cosmetic.
2. **Trim them off** for appearance. Use clean scissors and cut to match the natural shape of the leaf. Some people prefer a flat cut; others prefer to mimic the leaf's natural taper. Don't cut into healthy tissue.

Either way, treat the underlying cause or the next leaves will do the same thing.

## When brown tips mean something worse

If the brown is spreading rapidly (more than a few mm per week), is soft/mushy rather than crispy, or is accompanied by yellow halos around the brown, you have a fungal/bacterial issue, not a humidity issue. Improve airflow, stop misting, water only at the soil line, and consider a copper-based fungicide if it doesn't resolve.
