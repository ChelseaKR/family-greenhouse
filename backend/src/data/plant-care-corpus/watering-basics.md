# Watering houseplants — the actual rules

The biggest cause of houseplant death is overwatering, not underwatering. A wilted plant usually recovers within hours of being watered. A plant rotting from too much water often can't be saved.

## How to know when to water

Stick your finger an inch into the soil. If it feels dry, water. If it's still moist, wait. For pots too deep to reach the root zone, use a wooden chopstick or skewer — push it in, pull it out, and check if soil sticks to it. Damp soil sticks; dry soil doesn't.

Weight is the second-best signal. A dry pot feels noticeably lighter than a freshly-watered one. After a few cycles you can tell whether a plant needs water just by lifting the pot.

Avoid watering on a fixed calendar schedule. The right interval changes with the season, your home's humidity, the size of the pot, and how root-bound the plant is. A plant that needed water every 5 days in July might only need it every 14 in December.

## How to water properly

When you do water, water thoroughly: until water runs out the drainage holes. This rinses out salt buildup and ensures the entire root ball is hydrated, not just the top.

Empty the saucer 15-30 minutes after watering. Roots sitting in a puddle is the fastest path to rot.

Room-temperature water beats cold. Sudden cold can shock tropical roots. Letting tap water sit overnight also lets chlorine dissipate, which sensitive plants like calatheas appreciate.

## Bottom watering

For plants prone to leaf rot (African violets, gloxinias) or fungus gnats, bottom water: set the pot in a tray of water for 20-30 minutes, let the soil wick water up through the drainage holes, then drain. The top inch of soil stays dry, which suppresses fungus gnat larvae.

## Signs of overwatering vs underwatering

Overwatering and underwatering look similar — both cause drooping and yellow leaves. The tells:

- Overwatered: soil stays wet for days, leaves feel soft and limp, stems may be mushy at the base, lower leaves turn yellow first
- Underwatered: soil is bone-dry and pulling away from the pot edges, leaves feel crispy, the pot is very light

When in doubt, check the soil before assuming.
